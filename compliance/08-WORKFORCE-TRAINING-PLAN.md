# PalmCare AI — HIPAA Workforce Training Plan

**Policy Number:** WT-001
**Effective Date:** February 22, 2026
**Policy Owner:** Security Officer / CEO

---

## 1. Overview

All PalmCare AI workforce members (employees, contractors, interns) with access to PHI or systems that process PHI must complete HIPAA training within 30 days of onboarding and annually thereafter, as required by 45 CFR § 164.308(a)(5) and § 164.530(b).

---

## 2. Training Schedule

| Training Type | Audience | Frequency | Duration |
|--------------|----------|-----------|----------|
| HIPAA Basics | All workforce | Onboarding + Annual | 1 hour |
| Security Awareness | All workforce | Quarterly reminders | 15 min |
| Incident Response | All workforce | Annual | 30 min |
| Technical Security | Engineering team | Annual | 2 hours |
| Privacy Officer Training | Privacy Officer | Annual | 4 hours |

---

## 3. Core Training Topics

### Module 1: HIPAA Fundamentals (All Staff)
- What is HIPAA and why it matters
- What constitutes PHI (18 identifiers)
- Covered Entities vs. Business Associates
- PalmCare AI's role as a Business Associate
- Consequences of non-compliance (fines, termination, criminal penalties)

### Module 2: Privacy Rule Essentials (All Staff)
- Permitted uses and disclosures
- Minimum Necessary standard
- Individual rights (access, amendment, accounting)
- De-identification requirements
- When and how to report privacy concerns

### Module 3: Security Awareness (All Staff)
- Strong password practices
- Multi-factor authentication usage
- Recognizing phishing and social engineering
- Secure workstation practices (screen lock, clean desk)
- Reporting suspicious activity
- Safe handling of PHI in communications

### Module 4: Breach Response (All Staff)
- What constitutes a breach
- How to recognize a potential breach
- Immediate reporting requirements
- Who to contact (Security Officer)
- What NOT to do (don't investigate alone, don't delete evidence)

### Module 5: Technical Security (Engineering Team)
- Secure coding practices for PHI handling
- Encryption requirements (in transit and at rest)
- Logging do's and don'ts (never log PHI)
- Access control implementation
- Audit trail requirements
- Secure API design patterns
- Vendor security assessment

## 6. Ongoing Awareness

### Quarterly Security Reminders (Email)
- Q1: Password hygiene and MFA
- Q2: Phishing awareness and social engineering
- Q3: Incident reporting and breach scenarios
- Q4: Year-end review and policy updates

### Trigger-Based Training
Additional training required when:
- A security incident occurs
- Policies are significantly updated
- New systems are deployed that handle PHI
- Job role changes (new PHI access)
