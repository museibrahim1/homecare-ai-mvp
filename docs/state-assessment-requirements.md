## Overview & Federal Requirements

### OASIS (Outcome and Assessment Information Set)
- Required for **Medicare-certified** home health agencies
- OASIS-E1 effective January 1, 2025
- Applies to all patients regardless of payer (Medicare, Medicaid, managed care, private pay)
- Exceptions: patients under 18, maternity, chore/housekeeping only, single-visit patients
- Assessment timing: Start of Care, Resumption of Care, Recertification (every 60 days), Transfer, Discharge

### HCBS (Home and Community-Based Services)
- States use 1915(c) waivers, 1915(i) state plan, 1915(j) self-directed, or 1915(k) Community First Choice
- 124+ different functional assessment tools across 50 states
- CMS offers voluntary FASI (Functional Assessment Standardized Items) — 30-45 minutes to complete

### Universal Assessment Domains
All states evaluate some combination of:
- **ADLs** (Activities of Daily Living): Bathing, dressing, eating, toileting, transferring, ambulation
- **IADLs** (Instrumental ADLs): Meal prep, housekeeping, laundry, shopping, finances, medication management, transportation
- **Cognitive/Behavioral**: Memory, orientation, decision-making, behavioral concerns
- **Medical**: Diagnoses, medications, treatments, pain, skin integrity
- **Social/Environmental**: Living situation, support system, safety hazards

## State-by-State Guide

### Alabama

- **Medicaid Program**: Alabama Medicaid
- **HCBS Waivers**: Elderly & Disabled (E&D) Waiver, SAIL Waiver, Living at Home Waiver
- **Assessment Requirements**: Comprehensive needs assessment by RN or social worker; includes medical, functional, cognitive, and social evaluation. Uses state-developed Comprehensive Assessment Tool.
- **Assessment Domains**: ADLs, IADLs, cognitive status, behavioral health, medical conditions, living environment, informal supports
- **Reassessment Frequency**: Annual reassessment; more frequently if condition changes
- **Recording Consent**: One-party consent
- **Licensing**: Home health agencies licensed by Alabama Department of Public Health. Personal care agencies (non-medical) also require licensure.
- **Common Billing Codes**: T1019 (PCS per 15 min), S5125 (attendant care), S5130 (homemaker)
- **Key Regulations**: Alabama Admin Code Chapter 420-5-18 (Home Health Agencies)
- **Special Requirements**: Criminal background checks required; TB screening; 40-hour initial aide training

### Alaska

- **Medicaid Program**: Alaska Medicaid (Denali KidCare for children)
- **HCBS Waivers**: Adults with Physical Disabilities (APD), Community First Choice (1915(k))
- **Assessment Requirements**: Functional assessment using state Consumer Assessment Tool; determines level of care and service authorization
- **Assessment Domains**: ADLs (7 areas), IADLs (8 areas), cognitive function, behavioral needs, health conditions, environmental safety
- **Reassessment Frequency**: Annual reassessment required
- **Recording Consent**: One-party consent
- **Licensing**: Home and community-based services agencies licensed by DHSS. Personal care attendants must complete state-approved training.
- **Common Billing Codes**: T1019 (PCS), S5125 (attendant care)
- **Key Regulations**: 7 AAC 43 (Home and Community-Based Services)
- **Special Requirements**: Self-directed personal care option available; background check registry

### Arizona

- **Medicaid Program**: AHCCCS (Arizona Health Care Cost Containment System)
- **HCBS Waivers**: Arizona Long-Term Care System (ALTCS) — combines institutional and HCBS
- **Assessment Requirements**: Pre-Admission Screening (PAS) by ALTCS case manager; comprehensive functional assessment
- **Assessment Domains**: ADLs, IADLs, cognitive/behavioral status, medical needs, caregiver availability, risk assessment
- **Reassessment Frequency**: Annual reassessment; quarterly monitoring contacts
- **Recording Consent**: One-party consent
- **Licensing**: Home health agencies licensed by ADHS. Non-medical home care (attendant care) agencies need AHCCCS provider registration.
- **Common Billing Codes**: T1019 (attendant care), T1020 (per diem), S5130 (homemaker)
- **Key Regulations**: A.R.S. § 36-151 et seq.; AHCCCS Medical Policy Manual, Chapter 1200
- **Special Requirements**: AHCCCS is a managed care model; services authorized through managed care organizations. Fingerprint clearance card required.

### Arkansas

- **Recording Consent**: One-party consent
- **Licensing**: Home health agencies licensed by Arkansas Dept. of Health. Personal care agencies require DHS certification.
- **Common Billing Codes**: T1019 (PCS), S5125 (attendant care), S5130 (homemaker)
- **Key Regulations**: Arkansas Medicaid Provider Manual, Section 213; DHS Policy 1076
- **Special Requirements**: ARChoices uses independent assessment (not provider-performed); consumer direction option available

### California

- **Medicaid Program**: Medi-Cal
- **HCBS Waivers**: In-Home Supportive Services (IHSS), HCBS Waiver, Assisted Living Waiver, MSSP
- **Assessment Requirements**: Uniform Assessment Tool administered by county social workers for IHSS; OASIS for Medicare HHA
- **Assessment Domains**: ADLs, IADLs, cognition, mental health, medical needs, paramedical services, protective supervision
- **Reassessment Frequency**: Annual IHSS reassessment; more frequently as needed
- **Recording Consent**: **Two-party consent** (Cal. Penal Code § 632)
- **Licensing**: Home health agencies licensed by CDPH. IHSS (personal care) operates through counties, not private licensure. Home Care Organization (HCO) registration for non-medical home care.
- **Common Billing Codes**: T1019 (PCS), S5125, IHSS uses functional index ranking
- **Key Regulations**: Welf. & Inst. Code § 12300 et seq. (IHSS); HSC § 1725-1796.68 (Home Health); HSC § 1796.10 et seq. (Home Care Services)
- **Special Requirements**: Home Care Services Consumer Protection Act requires HCO registration, caregiver background checks, and DOJ/FBI fingerprinting. **Two-party consent for recording.**

## Summary: Key Takeaways for PalmCare AI

### Assessment Coverage by State
- **Currently Fully Implemented**: Nebraska (SLUMS, PHQ-2/9, NSI, ADL/IADL), Iowa (Form 470-4694 with 63 risk factors)
- **Priority Expansion**: States with highest home care market — California, Texas, New York, Florida, Pennsylvania, Illinois, Ohio
- **OASIS**: Required for all Medicare-certified HHAs nationwide — already covered by standard pipeline

### Recording Consent Implementation
- **13 Two-Party Consent States**: CA, CT, DE, FL, IL, MD, MA, MI, MT, NH, OR (in-person), PA, WA
- PalmCare AI's Twilio integration already handles consent notifications for these states
- For voice assessments, always obtain explicit consent before recording

### Billing Code Strategy
- **T1019** (PCS per 15 min) is the most universally used code across states
- **S5125** (attendant care) and **S5130** (homemaker) are common supplements
- State-specific modifiers are often required — always verify with state Medicaid
- PalmCare AI's billing extraction already maps to standard categories that align with these codes

### Next Steps for Knowledge Base Expansion
1. Embed state-specific assessment requirements into the AI prompt system
2. Add state selection to the assessment pipeline to automatically apply correct tools
3. Build state-specific billing code mapping tables
4. Integrate recording consent checks based on client/provider state
